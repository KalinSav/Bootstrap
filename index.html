Course Overview

Description

This is a 20-credit module consisting of two linked sub-sections – the first is ‘Evolution’ and the second is ‘Animal Biology’. In addition, there will be core statistics workshops embedded within the module.

Evolution and its product, organismal diversity, is the central theme of biology. Biology divides into four hierarchical levels: (i) the molecules that make up and regulate the structure of cells; (ii) the cells that make up the organisms; (iii) the organisms that make up these ecosystems; and (iv) the ecosystems that are the organisms interacting with each other and their environment.

Animals are a direct product of these evolutionary processes. The wide number of animal species, and their diverse adaptations, including complex behavioural strategies that allow species to persist through reproductive and survival strategies, can be understood in the context of evolution.

Aims & Learning Outcomes

Aims

  • To introduce the principles of natural selection in modern biology.
  • To provide a basic introduction to the diversity of different animal groups,
    covering both invertebrates and vertebrates.
  • To use practical classes and video sessions to develop skills in taxonomy,
    analysis and interpretation of empirical data, observing behaviour in
    natural populations and presenting experimental findings.

Learning Outcomes

At the end of the course the students will be able to:

  • discuss the development and current impact of evolutionary thought
  • demonstrate a working knowledge of the mechanisms of natural selection
  • recognise and recall taxonomic terms used in animal classification
